Welcome to RetirementOdds.com

 

This calculator will tell you your chances of having financial success in retirement. Typically, this means you don't run out of money in your lifetime(s), but could also mean not running out and also leaving a certain amount to your heirs.

It's important to know that retirement success is rarely a 100% certainty. Too much of the future is unknown, and there's no magic number for your net worth that means guaranteed success. We believe this so-called "FI Number" is the wrong way to look at the decision. Rather, you need make some reasonable assumptions, use them to determine your probability of success, and then decide what probability of success you are comfortable with. And you need to be prepared to make adjustments if things don't go as planned.

To determine your odds, we need to insert some unpredictability into the future of your money. For this, we use an average return on investment (ROI), and a standard deviation of ROI. Standard deviation is a measure of volatility. It's what makes your future returns unpredictable. If you don't know what that means, don't worry, we help you come up with these numbers by choosing a historical market time period to use as a predictor of the future. From there, you can always override as you see fit, or leave the suggestions as is.

When you press the Calculate button, we'll compute 1000 projections into the future, and tell you what percentage of them end in success. These are your Retirement Odds.

Interpreting Your Results

You should know that your results can vary widely based on your assumptions about the ever-unpredictable future, e.g. inflation, your return on invesment, and to some extent your spending. If you have all your investments in fixed-rate savings accounts, then your standard deviation is essentially 0% and your retirement odds will be either 0% or 100%. But that's no fun! Instead, most people will assume some risk in their investment portfolio to get better returns. When you do this, the tool lets you enter your risk level through the standard deviation inputs.

Once you have all your inputs figured out and entered into the tool, press "Calculate My Retirement Odds" to get your result. Now you get to decide if you're comfortable retiring based on these odds, or choosing to work longer for better odds. That's a very personal choice. How much risk are you comfortable with? As the developer of this tool, I used this methodology to decide to retire at the age of 54. My odds were about 90%. However, I probably would have been comfortable at even 80% or less, because I know I'll be tracking these odds all through my retirement years, and I have some backup plans if things start to go badly, such as being comfortable with reducing our spending. I also tried to use conservative, but realistic predictions about the future. You should too.

Remember, there's no "magic number" for financial success in retirement. When you acheive the odds you are comfortable with, and pull the trigger, it's time to start living your best life, on a budget!. Keep checking your status along the journey, and be prepared to make adjustments.

I built RetirementOdds.com because I couldn't find anything else out there that was easy to use, yet could still reproduce the details of my financial situation. I also didn't really trust anything that wouldn't show me the "under-the-hood" details of its calculations. And finally, while there are several ways to show results of a calculator, I felt that "odds of success" was the most informative for decision making purposes.
